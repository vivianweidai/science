<div class="section-heading"><h2>Overview</h2><span class="section-date">April 11th 2026</span></div>

Hands-on session with the centrifuge, pH meter, and essential lab equipment.

| Toolkit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Instruments | Thermo Scientific Sorvall RT3 Centrifuge, VWR pHenomenal pH 1100 L, Scientific Industries Vortex-Genie 2, Canlab Magnetic Stirrer, Sartorius CP225D Analytical Balance |
| Pipettes | Gilson Pipetman Pipettes |
| Tubes | Sarstedt 50 mL conical centrifuge tubes, Falcon 50 mL tubes |

## Samples

<div class="hero-single"><img src="photos/samples/samples1.jpeg" alt="Five labeled Sarstedt tubes: Coke, Milk, Yogurt, Apple Cider Vinegar, Vinegar"></div>

Five everyday liquids were loaded into labeled 50 mL centrifuge tubes. Each sample was vortexed, pH measured with the VWR electrode, then centrifuged.

| Sample | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Coke | Carbonated cola soft drink |
| Milk | Whole milk |
| Yogurt | Plain yogurt (liquid consistency) |
| Apple cider vinegar | Unfiltered apple cider vinegar |
| Vinegar | White distilled vinegar |

## Results

<div class="hero-single"><img src="photos/samples/samples8.jpeg" alt="Four labeled Sarstedt tubes before centrifugation: 1 Milk, 2 Yogurt, 3 Apple Cider Vinegar, 4 Milk + Vinegar"></div>

Four tubes were loaded into the Sorvall, balanced across the rotor (<a href="https://vivianweidai.com/research/projects/20260411%20Centrifuge/photos/data/data16.jpeg">Data 16</a>, <a href="https://vivianweidai.com/research/projects/20260411%20Centrifuge/photos/data/data17.jpeg">Data 17</a>). After the spin:

- **Tube 1 — Milk** — whey above, fat/casein pellet below (<a href="https://vivianweidai.com/research/projects/20260411%20Centrifuge/photos/data/data24.jpeg">Data 24</a>)
- **Tube 2 — Yogurt** — same, denser pellet (<a href="https://vivianweidai.com/research/projects/20260411%20Centrifuge/photos/data/data19.jpeg">Data 19</a>, <a href="https://vivianweidai.com/research/projects/20260411%20Centrifuge/photos/data/data20.jpeg">Data 20</a>)
- **Tube 3 — Apple cider vinegar** — "mother" pelleted, clearer vinegar above (<a href="https://vivianweidai.com/research/projects/20260411%20Centrifuge/photos/data/data18.jpeg">Data 18</a>)
- **Tube 4 — Milk + vinegar** — acid curdled casein into a pellet below clear whey (<a href="https://vivianweidai.com/research/projects/20260411%20Centrifuge/photos/data/data21.jpeg">Data 21</a>, <a href="https://vivianweidai.com/research/projects/20260411%20Centrifuge/photos/data/data22.jpeg">Data 22</a>, <a href="https://vivianweidai.com/research/projects/20260411%20Centrifuge/photos/data/data23.jpeg">Data 23</a>)

Centrifugation works best on emulsions and suspensions (milk, yogurt, unfiltered vinegar) where particles of different densities are physically mixed but not dissolved.
